Gray Wolves have repeatedly been put on and taken off the endangered species list in the state of Wisconsin, the most recent listing being in 1976. In 1999, the Wisconsin DNR implemented a wolf management plan, and by the end of January 2012, wolves were de-listed. Almost immediately, Senate Bill 411 was introduced to the Wisconsin state senate with intentions to open a hunting season, legalizing the hunting of Wolves. Three months later the bill passed and the first hunting season was set to begin in October 2012. This four month trapping, baiting, and hunting season was the first ever in the state. These are the results of the 2012-2013 season.
